About Me

I'm Joya Wheatfall-Melvin, a second-year Data Science in Public Policy candidate at Georgetown University's McCourt School of Public Policy, where I study how data and technology shape the lives of Black communities and the institutions that govern them. Driven by a commitment to data justice, I leverage data science to inform transformative policy and research that uplift communities across economic, education, and technology sectors. As a Tech & Public Policy Scholar and researcher for the Evidence for Justice Lab, I currently work on projects that analyze police enforcement patterns and AI systems used in all levels of the justice system.

Before Georgetown, I held research roles at Brown, Purdue, Spelman College, UVA, and Digital Promise, working on projects ranging from international democratic erosion to AI literacy in Southern school districts. Across these experiences, I've gathered a strong skillset in Python, R, SQL, and data visualization; further, deepening my commitment to using and analyzing data in ways to reveal truth, provide context, and support communities.
